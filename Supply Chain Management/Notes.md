## 1.1 What is Operations and Supply Chain Management

Historically: Companies simply took in inputs and produced outputs \
Now: Companies will look at where other companies are getting their inputs from (upstream) and where their outputs are going (downstream).

### 1.1.1 Operations and Supply Chain Management

Operations and supply chain managers focus on how to **develop capabilities to design, produce and deliver** products and services in a competitive market.

**Important!**: Supply chain management deals with *goods and services*. Not just physical products.

#### What kind of capabilities do OSCM managers develop?
* New products & service features
* Ways to decrease costs on delivering or producing goods/services
* Improvements in quality (eg. quality of product/production process/product delivery)
* Reducing time to market
* Ability to innovate

## 1.5 Responsive vs Efficient Supply Chain Models
### 1.5.1 The Supply Chain Operating Model

#### Two Types of Supply Chains:
1. **Efficient Supply Chains** are *cost-focused*:
    * Lower costs
    * Better utilization of assets
    * More efficient, productive
    * Better yield capacity
2. **Responsive Supply Chains** are *speed-focused*:
    * Faster response to changes in customer delivery requirements (eg. volume or lead time)
    * Shorter time to deliver products to customer

#### How Does a Company's Supply Chain Model Affect Overall Strategy?
||Efficient SC (Cost Focus)|Responsive SC (Speed Focus)|
|---|---|---|
|SC Operating Model|Supply the predicted demand efficiently at the lowest possible cost|Respond quickly to unpredictable demand in order to minimize OOS, lost sales, clearance discounts, obsolete inventory|
|Capacity Strategy|High utilization rate|Excess buffer capacity for flexibility to react to demand|
|Inventory Strategy|High turnover rate, minimized inventory stocks throughout the supply chain|Significant/extra stock as buffer to respond to sudden surges in demand|
|Lead Time Strategy|Reduce lead time as long as it doesn't increase costs|Invest aggressively in ways to reduce lead time|
|Supplier Capability|Select suppliers with better cost and quality|Select suppliers with better speed, flexibility, responsiveness|
|Logistics Strategy|Greater reliance on low cost modes (eg. shipping)|Greater reliance of fast modes of transport (eg. air freight)|

* Capacity: rate at which the company can product goods/services, how many people you can serve
* Utilization: percentage of how much capacity you are using
* Lead Time: Time between when a customer places an order to when they receive it.
* Logistics: Transport of stock

### 1.5.2 Why Have Different Models?
The type of product/service a company sells/provides dictates the type of supply chain model they might use.

#### Two Types of Products:
|Functional Products|Innovative Products|
|---|---|
|Relatively stable environment|Relatively unpredictable environment|
|Lower demand uncertainty|Higher demand uncertainty
|More predictable/stable demand|More unpredictable demand/difficulty in forecasting demand
|Longer product life|Shorter product life|
|Lower inventory cost|Higher inventory cost|
|Lower profit margins|Higher profit margins|
|Lower product variety & higher volume|Higher product variety and lower volume|
|Lower OOS and obsolescence cost<br>(Costs you less if customer wants the product and you don't have it)|Higher OOS and obsolescence cost<br>(Costs you more if customer wants the product and you don't have it)|

Functional products and innovative products need different supply chains.

*Functional* products need **Effcient Supply Chains**. \
*Innovative* products need **Responsive Supply Chains**.

### 1.5.3 Supply Chain Strategy (long-term) Decisions Drive Tactical (medium-term) and Operational (short-term) Decisions

#### Example of how the supply chain strategy might drive tactical and operational decision:
#### *Strategic* decision...
   * Efficient and low cost supply chain
#### *Tactically*, this means...
   * Low operating costs
   * High asset utilization/ROA 
#### *Operationally*, this means...
   * VMI - vendor managed inventory
   * DSD - direct store delivery (bypassing warehouses)
   * Full truckloads - delivering full truckloads instead of half a truck, make the most use out of transport

### 1.5.4 Summary
* Ultimate goal is to match supply of a product with the demand of that product
* Ideal supply chain model varies depending on the type of product
* Mixing both supply chains: agile supply chain

## 1.6 Network Configuration
#### Some components of a supply chain:
- Warehouses
- Suppliers
- Customers
- Markets

These components can exist within 1 region, or across multiple regions.

How do we configure the supply chain to meet different regional markets? For example, where should we place out warehouses and facilities?

### 1.6.1 Types of Network Configurations
#### Two Types of Network Configurations
1. Centralized Networks
2. Decentralized Networks

#### Considerations of a global supply chain network
Scenario: Your company has markets in multiple regions.
* Where should you put the warehouses to supply these markets?
* Where should you manufacture your products?
* Where should your suppliers be located?
* Where should you locate all of these components with respect to your customers?

|Solution|Description|
|---|---|
|Solution #1| - Suppliers, manufacturers, warehouses in 1 region<br>- From this 1 region, distribute globally<br>- Centralized sourcing, manufacturing, distribution|
|Solution #2|- Manufacture in 1 region<br>- Suppliers, warehouses in all key regions<br>- Suppliers, warehouses from any region can support demand in other regions<br>- Centralized manufacturing with global distribution|
|Solution #3|- Every region has its own suppliers, manufacturing, distribution<br>- Decentralized sourcing, manufacturing, distribution|
|Solution #4|- Global sourcing, manufacturing with decentralized distribution|

Most to Least Centralized:
1. Solution #1 (Most centralized)
2. Solution #2
3. Solution #4
4. Solution #3 (Least Centralized)

### 1.6.2 Advantages and Disadvantages 

|Centralized Network|Decentralized Network|
|---|---|
|**Advantages:**<br>- Less warehouses & inventory <br>- Higher pooling effect = Less need for safety stock<br>- Higher availability of inventory<br>- Higher shipment volume = Easier to negotiate for better deals<br>- Less complex, easier to coordinate<br>- Easier to standardize practices<br>- Higher economics of scale|**Advantages:**<br>- Less distance to customers<br>- Lower transportation cost and timing<br>- Increased responsiveness|
|**Disadvantages:**<br>- Further from customers<br>- Higher transportation cost and timing<br>- Decreased responsiveness|**Disadvantages:**<br>- More warehouses & inventory<br>- Less pooling effect = More need for safety stock<br>- Lower availability<br>- Lower shipment volume = Harder to negotiate for better deals<br>- More complex, harder to coordinate<br>- Harder to standardize practices<br>- Less economics of scale|

* Pooling effect: The effect of varying demand across regions is consolidated in the distribution region.
* Economics of Scale: Effectiveness in improving manufacturing in one area. Eg. In centralized manufacturing, improvements will affect 100% of your goods instead of in decentralized manufacturing where it might affect only 33% (1 region out of 3).

**TL;DR:** Advantages of a centralized supply chain are equal to the disadvantages of a decentralized supply chain, and vice versa.

### 1.6.3 When to choose a centralized or decentralized netowrk?
||Centralized Strategy|Decentralized Strategy|
|---|---|---|
|Demand Environment|Stable/predictable|Uncertain\Unpredictable|
|Velocity|Lower|Higher|
|Availability|Higher|Lower|
|Points of sale (delivery points)|Few|Many|
|Distribution cost per weight (Cost of distribution)|Lower|Higher|
|Responsiveness|Lower|Higher|
|Delivery lead times|Longer|Shorter|
|Delivery customization|Less|More|
|Supply Chain|Efficient supply chain|Responsive supply chain|

### 1.6.4 Summary
* Centralized network: Efficient supply chain, cost focused operating model
* Decentralized network: Responsive supply chain, speed focused operating model
